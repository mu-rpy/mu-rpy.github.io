About

I’m a systems-focused developer interested in the architecture of complex software, from distributed networks to low-level engine design.

My primary focus is game development (Unreal Engine & Godot), with an emphasis on networking and performance optimization.

I’ve built a custom engine from scratch to tackle specific challenges like implementing spatial partitioning for efficient collision detection and managing memory-safe rendering pipelines. My projects also included 3D modeling, rigging, and video editing to handle the full development cycle.

I enjoy pushing technical boundaries through experimental projects. This includes building a peer-to-peer mini-computer within Minecraft, where I had to solve for signal latency and logic-gate synchronization to simulate computation and basic networking in a restricted environment.

I use Python for rapid prototyping, but move to C# and Rust for performance-critical systems. My broader toolkit includes C, C++, TypeScript, and SQL, along with ongoing experimentation in Assembly to better understand hardware-level execution.
